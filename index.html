Full Time

Projects I'm actively managing are sponsored by The Department of Defense and other federal agencies. I'm the project lead and a full stack developer of the Propagation Modeling Website (PMW), which is a web-based signal propagation analysis tool. The PMW parallelizes low-level Fortran and C propagation models, such as the IRI, produces intermediate results, processes them, stores them and retrieves them.

I wrote a good portion of a Radio Frequency Coordination Portal (RFCP) for the winners of the 1695-1710 MHz spectrum auction. This includes large swaths of the user authentication, email services, and other administrative subsystems.

Most of this codebase is using .NET, ArcObjects (from Esri) and various other Microsoft frameworks, but I'm used to developing in Linux as well as OS X.

Last couple of years I've participated as a US delegation chairman in the ITU-R in beautiful Geneva, Switzerland. My contributions primarily focus upon refractivity datasets derived from global reanalysis data, and propagation fundamentals.
